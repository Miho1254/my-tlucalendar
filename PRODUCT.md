# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

adaptive

## Users

Thuy Loi University students (primarily Vietnamese, ages 18–24). They open the app multiple times daily — sometimes a quick 3-second glance to check today's schedule, sometimes a longer session to plan the semester, review grades, or simulate GPA targets. They are time-poor, mobile-first, and expect the tool to just work without explanation.

## Product Purpose

A free, open-source utility that replaces the university's clunky web portal with a fast, offline-capable mobile app. It syncs schedules, exams, grades, tuition, and course registration data, then presents it through a clean interface that respects the student's time. Success is measured by daily active usage and the absence of "I didn't know about that exam" moments.

## Brand Personality

Efficient, utilitarian, no-nonsense. The app is a tool, not a toy. Voice is direct and brief — Vietnamese first, never stilted translation. Tone is competent and slightly casual, like a reliable senior student who knows the system. Not playful, not corporate, not sterile.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI templates** — cream/beige backgrounds, identical card grids with icon+heading+text, gradient text, glassmorphism, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section. The saturated 2025–2026 AI look that screams "generated, not designed."
- **Overwhelming dashboards** — data overload with too many charts, cards, and colors competing for attention. A wall of information with no hierarchy.
- **Childish/toy-like UI** — excessive rounded corners, cartoonish icons, playful animations. Too casual for a university tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Data-first, not decoration-first** — every pixel serves information density or clear navigation. No decorative elements that don't carry meaning.
2. **Speed as UX** — offline-first with cache-first loading, instant feedback, no loading screens when data is available locally.
3. **Respect the student's time** — no friction, no surprise, no tutorial needed. The interface should be self-evident on first use.
4. **Confident simplicity** — no hand-holding, no excessive explanation. The app assumes a capable user and gets out of the way.
5. **Vietnamese-native, not translated** — idioms, greetings, and layout all feel natively Vietnamese. No awkward English-first phrasing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance as baseline. The OLED dark theme already provides strong contrast. Ensure reduced-motion support for all animations, minimum touch target sizes (48x48dp), and screen reader compatibility for core navigation flows. No specific user needs beyond standard accessibility practice.
